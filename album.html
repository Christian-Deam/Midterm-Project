Iowa State Buildings

Here are some of the well-known buildings of Iowa State!

The ISU Public Library is a place for students to go to quietly work and study while on campus.

Official Website Hours

The Memorial Union is a public place with multitudes of student services and activities.

Official Website Events About

The Student Innovation Center is a place where students imaginate, create, and of course innovate.

Official Website Innovation Programs

Beardshear Hall is the place that new students should become familiar with as that's where most of their in-person registration is.

Human Resources Financial Aid

Maple, Willow, and Larch are all residence halls perfect for incoming students.

Maple Residence Willow Residence Larch Residence

The State Gym is great centralized place for students to work out and exercise.

The Lied Rec Center is another gym placed to the north of Maple, Willow, and Larch, a good starting gym for those new students.

In the center of campus lies the Campanile. Being one of the major symbols of Iowa State as well as the univervisty's alma mater.

Curtiss Hall, first called Agriculture Hall, is the hub of Agricultural knowledge and exams.

Simon Estes Music Hall is the place where music majors gather to learn. Additionally, concerts and final performances of the students are played here as well.

Jack Trice Stadium is the place to go for football games. Eager fans pack the seats and pridefully cheer for their side.

Hilton Coleseum is the place to go to see the upcoming basketball game.

The most matching place for a musical menagerie to monger and mingle or man and mouse to meet and mask onstage might materialize at Stephens Auditorium.
